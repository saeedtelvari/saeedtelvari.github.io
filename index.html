About Me

I'm Sa'eed Telvari, a PhD candidate in Petroleum Engineering at Heriot-Watt University, working under the supervision of Dr. Florian Doster. My research bridges the gap between computational efficiency and physical accuracy in subsurface flow simulation.

My journey began at Amirkabir University of Technology (Tehran Polytechnic), where I completed both my B.Sc. and M.Sc. in Petroleum Engineering with a focus on reservoir simulation and machine learning applications. I was granted direct admission for graduate study based on my academic excellence, ranking within the top 2% in the national entrance exam.

Currently, I'm developing Vertical Equilibrium (VE) models for simulating CO2 storage in depleted gas reservoirs—a key simulation strategy for achieving net-zero emissions. My work combines numerical methods, machine learning, and domain expertise to create computationally efficient yet accurate simulation tools for Carbon Capture, Utilization, and Storage (CCUS).

Research Interests

CO2 Storage Modeling

Developing efficient numerical models for CO2 injection and storage in depleted reservoirs, focusing on Vertical Equilibrium approaches for large-scale simulations.

Reservoir Simulation

Advanced simulation techniques using Eclipse and MRST for complex reservoir systems, including fractured and heterogeneous formations.

Upscaling Methods

Machine learning-enhanced upscaling of fine-scale discrete fracture models to field-scale simulations while preserving flow characteristics.

Digital Rock Analysis

Pore-scale modeling and 3D CNN-based prediction of petrophysical properties from micro-CT images of rock samples.

Depleted Oil/Gas Reservoirs

Characterization and modeling of depleted hydrocarbon reservoirs for CO2 storage, including residual gas effects and multi-phase flow dynamics.

Machine Learning in PE

Applying deep learning and neural networks to accelerate reservoir characterization and property prediction workflows.
